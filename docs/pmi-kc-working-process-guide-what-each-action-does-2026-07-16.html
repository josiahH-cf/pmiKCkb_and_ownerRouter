The system in plain English

Work moves by a simple human-controlled pattern

The application gathers source-backed facts, makes the next decision visible, and records what the human chose. It performs an external effect only when that exact action is enabled and exactly confirmed.

1 · NoticeA task, question, flag, message, or ticket needs attention.
2 · OpenThe user opens the owning record—not just a summary count.
3 · UnderstandThe app gathers permitted facts, sources, history, and missing information.
4 · DecideA human resolves, approves, returns, assigns, or chooses the next step.
5 · ActThe app records its own state or performs one separately enabled exact effect.
6 · ProveThe result appears as history, a receipt/readback, a new attention item, or completion.

Information is missing

The app should show what is missing, where the fact should come from, and the next person or surface that can supply it. It should not fill the gap with a confident guess.

Sources disagree

The app shows the candidates and their sources. A human chooses the trusted source, enters a correction, or rejects the flag, with the required reason.

An external result is unclear

The app does not try again blindly. It checks the provider result or Message-ID and asks for reconciliation if certainty cannot be restored.

Lease Renewal · intended operating process

One lease should move through one understandable lifecycle

PMI KC’s documented business process begins with a lease that may need renewal and ends only after the chosen path, documents, provider updates, and evidence are accounted for. This is the operating process the application is meant to support; it is not a claim that every step is currently connected in one Live screen.

🧠 Intended process, grounded in the active Lease product documentation The app should carry the same lease identity, source facts, decisions, blockers, receipts, and activity through the journey. Where sources disagree, the human resolution becomes part of that record; it does not silently replace source data.
1

Find the right leases

Begin the documented default cadence roughly two months before lease end. Identify candidates from RentVine timing, include off-cycle cases and exceptions, and log the work in the team’s Lease Renewal Sheet.

Outcome: a bounded cohort with a reason each lease is in, out, or needs review.

2

Establish the facts

Reconcile RentVine lease facts and current rent with the Sheet worklog. Use the PMI Free Rental Analysis for the authoritative market number and Zillow for range/context; Zillow plus manual approval is the fallback. Missing or contradictory values become explicit flags.

Outcome: a source-backed owner package, not a guessed recommendation.

3

Get owner direction

Present timing, current terms, market context, recommendation, and unresolved issues. Record whether the owner will renew and the approved terms.

Outcome: one human-approved direction that governs tenant outreach.

4

Contact the tenant

After owner approval, deliver the tenant offer by the documented 15th-of-the-month target while leaving at least 30 days to sign. Use email, RentVine Portal Chat, and SMS; a person confirms each channel and each effect needs its own receipt.

Outcome: tenant response or a visible follow-up state, with channel evidence.

5

Follow the chosen branch

A non-renewal enters Move-Out. A renewal gathers deposit, inspection, pet, insurance, charge, occupancy, and property facts. Tenant agreement comes before conditional document build-out.

Outcome: the correct next process and conditional document set.

6

Sign, update, and close

Build the approved Dotloop package, track signatures, apply the signed renewal through the exact RentVine contract, verify applicable services/charges, update the Sheet closeout state, and reconcile receipts.

Outcome: a completed renewal whose final state can be proven.

TENANT WILL NOT RENEW

Move-Out branch

Start the documented Move-Out process, preserve the renewal decision and message evidence, and stop renewal-document actions.

Tenant decision
TENANT WILL RENEW

Renewal branch

Verify conditional facts, assemble the approved documents, obtain signatures, update systems, and close with receipts.

What each system appears to own in the intended Lease process
RentVine · lease facts, timing, parties, current rent Renewal Sheet · team worklog and decisions Market sources · pricing context, manually reviewed Dotloop · document package and signed lease Gmail / Portal / SMS · separate communication evidence PMI KC app · workflow, resolutions, approvals, receipts, audit

The Sheet is operationally important, but it is reconciled rather than trusted blindly. The app should keep backlinks and evidence; it should not pretend to be the authoritative lease ledger or signed-document repository.

Model-readable instructions for interpreting the Lease process
  1. Begin the documented default cadence roughly two months before lease end, while still capturing off-cycle and exceptional cases, and preserve the lease identity through every step.
  2. Load current rent and lease facts from RentVine; use the PMI Free Rental Analysis for the authoritative market number and Zillow for range and context. When the PMI analysis is unavailable, Zillow plus manual review and approval is the documented fallback.
  3. Show source conflicts to a person and record the selected source, correction, rejection, and reason.
  4. Do not prepare tenant terms until owner direction is recorded. Under the documented default cadence, deliver the tenant offer by the 15th while leaving at least 30 days to sign.
  5. Treat email, Portal Chat, and SMS as separate actions with separate confirmation and evidence.
  6. Branch non-renewals out of the renewal path; do not continue document or writeback actions.
  7. Do not begin document build-out until the tenant agrees. Then build only the conditional documents supported by verified property, occupancy, pet, insurance, deposit, and charge facts.
  8. Invoke a Live provider action only when that exact contract is enabled; otherwise preserve the workflow and expose the unavailable action honestly.
  9. Track signatures, update the Renewal Sheet closeout state, and close only after every applicable enabled effect is reconciled to a receipt or readback.
❓ Business details still require human verification The evidence does not fully settle every exception cadence, the exact RentVine renewal/rent-increase navigation, every inherited-lease document variant, or every building-level field. These are verification targets—not permission to invent a step.

Decision language

Resolve, approve, and execute are different events

Several controls sit next to one another but authorize different things. The safe, usable interpretation is: first decide what is true, then authorize a specific next state, then—only if separately enabled—perform one exact external effect and prove it.

ReviewSee the record, candidates, source, risk, and target.
ResolveRecord which fact or disposition a human accepts.
ApproveAuthorize one exact proposed transition or effect.
ExecuteCall only the separately enabled action after exact confirmation.
ReconcileBind the result to a receipt/readback or preserve ambiguity.
ContinueAdvance the owning workflow and show the next human action.
APP ONLY

Resolve

Chooses a source, correction, rejection, or disposition and records the reason. It changes app-owned interpretation, not the source record.

APP ONLY BY DEFAULT

Approve

Authorizes a proposal or transition. Unless the control explicitly says execution is included and eligible, approval does not contact a provider.

POSSIBLE LIVE EFFECT

Execute

Performs one exact action only after preview and confirmation. Target, effect, identity, idempotency, receipt/readback, and rollback behavior belong to that action.

EXCEPTION CONTROL

Reconcile

Used when an attempted effect cannot be proven successful or failed. It prevents a blind retry from duplicating a message or write.

WORKFLOW STATE

Complete a step

Records checklist progress. Completion proves the app step was marked; it proves a provider effect only when a matching provider receipt is also attached.

WORKFLOW STATE

Skip / return / snooze

Preserves why work did not advance and who should see it next. It is a controlled state transition, not a deletion of the underlying need.

✅ What an explicit write should tell the human Before confirmation: the action, provider, exact target, proposed effect, current value when applicable, risk, data mode, and whether it can be reversed. Afterward: success/failure/ambiguity, attempt ID, provider receipt or readback, timestamp, and the next action. If those are not present, do not infer that a write happened.
Concrete examples in the current application
  • Lease “Resolve”: persists a source decision and queues an app proposal; it does not change the Sheet.
  • Lease “Approve write-back”: persists app authorization; the implemented path still does not change the Sheet.
  • Test “Run Test action”: creates an internal Test receipt; the named provider is not constructed or contacted.
  • Gmail exact reply/label: can reach the signed-in user’s mailbox only from a linked Live workflow after exact confirmation and returns evidence.
  • Maintenance “Create ticket”: creates the app record; it does not imply a RentVine/LeadSimple/provider work order exists.

Source systems provide operational facts

The PMI KC app owns coordination

Firestore/app state holds runs, tickets, flags, resolutions, approvals, action attempts, receipts, attention, and audit suitable for the product workflow.

Console and Notifications are projections

They point to work and may expose bounded actions; the underlying Lease run, ticket, task, or condition remains the owning record.

Approval and Communications are control surfaces

Human review pass

Use the app, then correct this interpretation

This checklist is deliberately action-oriented. A reviewer can use invented data through the application’s dedicated Test controls, compare the visible consequence with the intended operating process, and mark precisely where the interpretation or implementation differs. A control documented as Live should not be repurposed for Test entry.

How to review without conflating modes Keep SAMPLE, TEST, APP ONLY, and LIVE visible in every note. A Test receipt can prove workflow behavior; it cannot prove that Gmail, RentVine, Sheets, Dotloop, SMS, Boom, or another provider was contacted.
🧭 Console and process start
  • Ask a plain-language question without a slash command; verify a cited answer or explicit source uncertainty appears.
  • Choose a process and start a Test run; verify the new run is labeled Test and no external action is implied.
  • Create an eligible Capture Task; verify the uncertainty becomes owned work rather than disappearing.
  • Follow one Console/Notification attention link; verify it lands on the record that can actually resolve the need.
🏘️ Lease Renewal
  • Open a sample lease; confirm every value and action is clearly labeled as invented/sample and nothing persists.
  • Prepare an owner and tenant email; confirm both are preview-only and no Gmail draft/send is suggested.
  • As Admin, open Live Review; confirm the sources read, reconciliation flags, and scope are understandable.
  • Resolve a flag; confirm the app shows the selected fact, reason, actor, and that neither source changed.
  • Approve the proposed write-back; confirm the UI does not claim a Sheet/RentVine write occurred.
  • Create a persistent Test renewal; progress each lifecycle state and verify action dependencies/confirmations.
  • Run all eleven Test actions; verify one internal receipt per action, no duplicate attempt, no provider contact, and Done only after all receipts.
  • Compare the resulting run with the documented business lifecycle: owner decision, tenant response, documents, signatures, provider updates, and closeout should be traceable—or explicitly identified as an unconnected step.
🛠️ Maintenance and Vendor
  • Verify the normal capture explicitly creates a Live-mode app record; do not use that control for invented Test scenarios.
  • Click Create Test ticket; run the six staff-side Test actions with exact confirmations and verify one internal receipt per action, with no provider contact.
  • On the Test ticket, change assignee, add a note, move through waiting/scheduled states, and close with a reason; confirm history and filters update.
  • Promote and dismiss safe Test intake examples; confirm only promotion creates an operational ticket.
  • Complete the canonical Test Vendor assignment; confirm the Vendor sees only its assigned matching-mode ticket, then separately test draft, label, and exact simulated-reply controls in the Vendor mailbox.
  • On an authorized Live ticket, exercise a safe linked Gmail read/label/reply with exact confirmation; verify receipt or explicit ambiguity.
✅ Approval, communication, connection, and administration
  • For each approval control, state before clicking whether it changes app state, performs an external action, or both; confirm the result matches.
  • Verify Communications exposes workflow links/health rather than a general inbox or compose surface.
  • Verify a linked Gmail panel cannot act on an unrelated thread or cross Test/Live mode.
  • Run bounded connection verification and confirm no credential-entry UI or hidden write is implied.
  • Change a safe Test staff role/scope only through an authorized Admin ceremony; confirm the reason and audit are visible.
  • Provision/reset/disable the canonical Test Vendor; confirm identity rotation/session revocation/TOTP reset behavior stays Test-only and fail-closed.
📝 Questions the review should answer—not assumptions this guide makes
  • Is the documented Lease order the order PMI KC staff actually follow, including exceptions and timing?
  • Which specific screen should own the single connected Live renewal lifecycle?
  • At each handoff, is the next person, due date, blocker, and exact next action visible?
  • Are source authority and correction rules correct for each Lease field?
  • Do owner and tenant decisions branch the process at the right moment?
  • Are the required conditional documents, charges, services, and closeout evidence complete?
  • Which remaining maintenance operating steps belong in the app versus an external system?
  • Does every label that sounds like a write accurately distinguish authorization from actual execution?
