Your app: everything it does, in plain language

A guide for PMI KC Metro. Updated 2026-07-23.

This is your team's operations app. Instead of ten browser tabs and a spreadsheet, your team works from one question box and a handful of clear desks: renewals, maintenance, approvals, and communications, with the property data already attached. The idea you will notice throughout is simple: the app does the drafting and the checking, and a person always makes the decision and presses the button. This guide walks through what it does, how to use it, and a little of how it works behind the scenes.

1. Signing in and who can use it

Open the app in any web browser and sign in with your PMI KC Metro Google work account (the pmikcmetro.com account). That is the only way in, and it is the same sign-in your team already uses for email. Once you are in, you land on the Console, the app's home screen, with a menu across the top.

What each person can do depends on their role:

  • Editor prepares the work, such as renewal and maintenance drafts.
  • Approver reviews and approves the items that need a second set of eyes.
  • Admin manages people, connections, and settings, and can do everything above. Today you and Dan are the Admins.

People can also be scoped to just renewals or just maintenance, so each person sees the work that is theirs. If someone steps away, the app warns them and then signs them out on its own to keep things safe.

2. Ask anything (the Console) Everyone

The home screen and the front door to everything. One box, plain English, trustworthy answers.

What it does

  • One question box. Your team types a real question in plain English, such as "What is the lease renewal workflow?" and gets a clear answer. No menus to learn.
  • Answers you can trust. Answers come from your approved knowledge, and each one cites its sources so anyone can check the work. Many answers include step-by-step handling instructions, not just a paragraph.
  • It will not guess. When there is no approved source for a question, the app says so plainly and points you to who owns the answer, instead of making something up. That honesty is the foundation of everything else.
  • It shows its work. Every answer says which assistant wrote it and how many sources back it, and shows when each source was last reviewed. If two sources disagree, it flags the conflict for a person to settle rather than quietly picking one.
  • It covers your whole operation. Answers draw from every area that has knowledge loaded, renewals, maintenance, move-ins and move-outs, owner onboarding, and more, and each answer names the area it came from.
  • It knows which process you mean. Type "broken hvac needs repair" and it recognizes the maintenance work-order process and offers to start it.
  • Practice mode. Your team can rehearse any workflow from start to finish as a test run, clearly labeled, with nothing real happening and nothing sent.
  • Speak your question. A dictate button lets you ask by voice.
  • It tells you what needs you. Below the box, a "what needs your attention" panel surfaces the three things that actually need a person: decisions to make, connections to finish, and coverage gaps.

3. Spaces: the map of your operation Everyone

A single screen that shows every area the app covers.

What it does

  • A tile for each area of your business: lease renewals, owner renewal outreach, tenant renewal notices, maintenance work orders, vendor handoffs, daily inbox triage, escalation rules, move-ins, move-outs and deposits, owner onboarding, and workflow communications.
  • Status at a glance. A colored dot on each tile tells you whether that area is ready to run, still needs a connection, or is planned for later.
  • Pick a desk. The working areas open straight into their own desk; reference areas open material your team can read.

4. Lease renewals Renewals access

The flagship. It turns a renewal window into a guided, step-by-step desk that catches data problems, routes decisions to a person, and drafts the emails for you to send.

What it does

  • It triages for you. The desk sorts the leases in your renewal window into what to work now, what needs a second look, and what it set aside, with the reason. Your team does not open a spreadsheet and guess where to start.
  • Every lease shows the next step. Each lease carries a four-step progress bar (data check, owner decision, tenant offer, build documents) and a single clear "next" action, so there is never a "what was I doing on this one?" moment.
  • It checks your data and catches conflicts. For each lease it compares your two rent sources, RentVine and the tracking sheet, field by field, and marks whether they agree, disagree, or are missing. It never dresses an unconfirmed number up as verified.
  • A person settles disagreements, with a reason. When the sources disagree, the app asks a person to choose the right value and write down why, and it keeps that decision in a permanent, plain-English history.
  • Market comps, your numbers. You record comps from comparable listings and they appear with their source right in the owner draft. The app can suggest a renewal rent from those comps, and an Admin approves that exact number before it ever goes into a draft.
  • It drafts the messages. It prepares the owner and tenant messages across email, portal chat, and text, each clearly marked "review before sending."
  • Real drafts, only to verified people. On a real lease it can create a ready-to-review draft in your own Gmail, addressed to the tenant or owner pulled straight from RentVine. If it cannot confirm exactly who the message is going to, it will not create the draft. A person opens Gmail and presses Send.
  • It remembers where each lease is. Record the owner's decision once and the app carries it forward: it builds the tenant offer from that number, tracks exactly where each lease is, and lets you mark it complete. No re-typing the rent, no guessing which renewals you have already handled.
  • A safe sample desk. A sample version of the desk lets your team learn the whole workflow before touching live data.

5. The approval queue Renewals access

One place every decision funnels through, with the right person, a reason, and a permanent record. This is how you stay in control as volume grows.

What it does

  • One decision inbox. Everything across the app that needs a yes or no collects here, most urgent first. One place to look; nothing slips.
  • Friction that matches the risk. Routine items approve in one click. Anything high-risk asks you to confirm and write down why, and that reason is required and recorded.
  • No silent no. Deny, send back for revision, or defer, and each one records a reason so the other person knows what to fix.
  • The right person decides. You cannot approve your own assigned work, and the most consequential actions require an Admin. The person who prepares a thing is not the person who approves it.
  • Fast in bulk, safe by design. You can act on many items at once, within a sensible limit, and even bulk actions cannot fire a mass external effect.

6. Maintenance Maintenance access

From a plain-English report to a tracked ticket, moved through its life with reasons and history.

What it does

  • Capture in plain English. Your team types what the tenant said, picks the unit, and the app writes the work order: a summary, an inferred priority, and even a suggested trade. One click turns it into a tracked ticket.
  • No half tickets. It will not create a ticket that is missing an address or a description, so you never chase a work order with no location on it.
  • A full life with a paper trail. Move a ticket from open to closed and back, with a required reason at each step. Assignments are checked against your real team, and the history reads like a plain-English log, so if an owner asks "what happened with that leak in March," the answer is right there.
  • Hand it to a vendor. You can assign a ticket to a vendor and track the exchange, and practice the whole handoff safely before anything real goes out.
  • A holding area for outside requests. Requests that arrive from a tenant link land in a holding area first. A person promotes the real ones and dismisses the noise; nothing untrusted becomes a work order on its own.
  • Owner notice, drafted for you. A ticket can prepare an owner-notice email addressed to the property owner from RentVine, ready for a person to review and send.
Open Maintenance

7. Communications Everyone

A focused, governed lane for the workflow emails the app helps with. Everyday inbox work stays in Gmail, and the app says so plainly.

What it does

  • It drafts replies for you. It writes the reply and marks anything it is unsure of as "needs verification" so nothing is invented, and hands you the text to review.
  • It knows its limits. For the most sensitive topics, owner money, legal notices, and tenant disputes, it does not draft at all. Those always go through a person.
  • It summarizes long threads. It can read a long email thread and tell you what it is about, who is waiting, and what to do next, as a suggestion, so you can clear a backlog fast.
  • One careful, confirmed send. When you do send, it is an exact reply to a thread already linked to a real ticket, the recipient and message are rebuilt from the real thread, and a person checks a box that says "I reviewed the recipient and the message" first. It sends once, and a network hiccup cannot make it send twice.
  • No blast button anywhere. There is no "email everyone" control to find. The app cannot email your owners or tenants on its own; every message is a person's decision.
Open Communications

8. Notifications Everyone

A live tally of what needs a person, without becoming noisy.

What it does

  • A personal to-do count. A bell at the top of every page shows how many things need you, and even the browser tab title carries the count. It refreshes on its own.
  • Click straight to the item. Opening a notification takes you to the exact thing that needs attention and marks it read.
  • Tune it to you. Each person can mute the types they do not need, and the setting sticks across sessions.
  • Set-up reminders that mean it. Reminders to finish a setup step stay until the thing is actually fixed, so they are not dismissible noise.
Open Notifications

9. Setup, roles, and admin Admin

How you run and maintain the app: who can do what, the audit trails, and connecting your systems.

What it does

  • Manage your team. Change a teammate's role with a required reason, and the change is recorded in an audit log and takes effect on their next sign-in.
  • See what is happening. An activity and logs area shows the approval-queue depth, feedback, and access changes.
  • Review feedback. Anyone can send feedback with the "report an issue" button on any page. It files to a review list, and a short internal notice goes to your staff. It never emails a tenant or owner.
  • Grow the app. Request a new knowledge area, and the app records the request and prepares the exact setup steps for you.
  • Connect your systems. The Connection Center shows which outside tools are connected and healthy, and is where new tools come online as you provide access.
  • Cost control built in. The app runs under a small spending cap with alerts and a real, billing-level shut-off, so cost can never run away.

10. How it works behind the scenes

You do not need any of this to use the app, but here is the short version of what is under the hood.

  • Your real data, read safely. The app reads your leases and contacts from RentVine and compares them against your Google tracking sheet. RentVine is treated as the trusted source; the app reads it and never quietly changes it.
  • Grounded answers. The question box is powered by Google's Gemini assistant, kept honest by Vertex AI Search over your approved documents, so answers are grounded in your own material and cite it.
  • Drafts in your own Google Workspace. Renewal and maintenance drafts are created in your own Gmail, so a person reviews and sends them from the mailbox they already use.
  • A permanent record. Decisions, approvals, and status changes are written to an add-only history, so nothing is lost and every action has a name, a time, and a reason.
  • Runs on Google Cloud. The app is hosted on Google Cloud with your company identity, behind sign-in, with the spending cap and shut-off described above.

11. You are always in control

The single promise that runs through the whole app: it drafts and it checks, but a person always decides. No email or message goes to an owner, tenant, or vendor unless a person reviews the exact message and confirms it. The app can prepare a draft, but a human presses Send. Only small notices to your own staff go out on their own. Your systems of record stay under your control, sensitive money and legal topics always route to a person, and a real spending cap stays on the whole time.
