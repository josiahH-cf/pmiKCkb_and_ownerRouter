Your app: what it does and how to use it

A plain-language guide for PMI KC Metro. Updated 2026-07-23.

This is your team's web app for lease renewals, maintenance, and answering questions from a trusted knowledge base. You open it in a normal web browser and sign in with your PMI work account. This guide explains how to get in, who can use it, and what each part of the menu does, in simple terms.

How to sign in and who can

Open the app and you land on the sign-in page. Sign in with your PMI KC Metro Google work account (the pmikcmetro.com account). Personal Google accounts are not allowed, and the app never uses one.

  1. Go to the sign-in page.
  2. Choose your PMI work Google account and continue.
  3. You arrive at the Console, the app's home screen.

Who can use it. Everyone on the team uses the same work-account sign-in. What you can see and do depends on your role:

  • Editor prepares work, such as renewal drafts.
  • Approver reviews and approves items that need a second set of eyes.
  • Admin manages people, connections, and settings, and can do everything above. Today you and Dan are the Admins.

If you are ever away from the keyboard for a while, the app warns you and then signs you out on its own to keep things safe.

The main menu

Across the top of every page is the menu. From left to right you see the pmi. name (which takes you home), then the tabs below, then a bell for notifications, your role, and a sign-out button. You only see the tabs your role and access allow.

Console Everyone

The home screen. Also opens when you click "pmi." or "Console."

The Console is your starting point. It shows what needs your attention and has an Ask box where you can type a question in plain English (for example, about a policy or a process) and get an answer backed by your approved knowledge sources. Each answer shows how it was produced and how many sources it used, so you can trust it. From here you can also start a task, such as preparing a renewal notice, and the app walks you through the safe steps.

Open the Console

Spaces Everyone

A front-door menu of your work areas.

Spaces is a simple menu of the different areas the app covers, such as Renewals and Maintenance. Pick a Space to jump into that area. Think of it as choosing which desk you want to sit at.

Open Spaces

Lease Renewal Renewals access

Prepare and track lease renewals.

This is the renewal desk. It reads real lease details, helps you prepare the renewal notice to the owner or the tenants, and tracks where each renewal stands. You can record the rent basis from comparable listings, and the notice shows that basis with its sources. When a rent number is suggested, an Admin has to approve that exact number before it goes into a draft. Nothing is sent from here on its own: the app prepares the draft and a person reviews and sends it.

Open Lease Renewal

Maintenance Maintenance access

Track maintenance tickets and owner notices.

The maintenance area tracks repair tickets and helps you prepare the notice to the property owner about a maintenance item. As with renewals, the app prepares the draft and a person reviews and sends it. Tenant photos and details are handled carefully and are never mixed into the knowledge base.

Open Maintenance

Approval Queue Renewals access

One place for items that need a decision.

The Approval Queue is a single, urgent-first list of items waiting for a decision, so nothing slips through the cracks. You open an item, see the detail, and approve or resolve it right there. Higher-risk items ask for a short written reason before you approve, which is kept for the record.

Open the Approval Queue

Communications Everyone

Workflow emails, drafts, and templates.

Communications is where workflow email lives: the renewal and maintenance drafts, the message templates, and replies tied to a renewal or a ticket. It only touches messages linked to your work here; it is not a general inbox. Every send is reviewed and confirmed by a person first.

Open Communications

Connections Everyone (Admin sets up)

See which outside tools are connected.

Connections shows the outside tools the app can work with, such as RentVine and Google Sheets, and whether each one is connected and healthy. Everyone can see the status. An Admin does the actual setup. This is where a new tool becomes available once you provide its key or access.

Open Connections

Admin Admin only

People, settings, feedback, and behind-the-scenes tools.

The Admin area is for managing the app. From here an Admin manages who is on the team and their roles, reviews feedback that people send with the "Report an issue" button, and reaches the setup and review tools. Only Admins see this tab.

Open Admin

The bell, feedback, and sign out

  • The bell (notifications). The bell near the top right collects what needs attention, in time order. Click it to open your notifications.
  • Report an issue. A button is on every page. If something looks wrong or you have an idea, click it and write a short note. It goes straight to the team for review, and your staff destination also gets a short internal heads-up. It never emails a tenant or owner.
  • Sign out. The sign-out button is at the top right. The app also signs you out on its own after a period of no activity.

Our safety promise. No email or message is ever sent to an owner, tenant, or vendor unless a person reviews the exact message and confirms it. The app can prepare a draft, but a human always presses send. Only small internal notices to your own staff can send on their own. The app keeps a safe practice mode so a mistaken click can never send a real message, and a real spending cap stays switched on at all times.

A note on branding. This guide and the app use the confirmed PMI name "pmi." and tagline, on the black-and-orange identity from your brand book cover. The official PMI logo files, exact brand color values, and brand fonts are not yet in our files, so the app currently uses clean, accessible default styling and does not guess a logo or a color. When you send the official brand files, we will wire in the real logo, colors, and fonts. This is listed as an open item in the unblock guide.
