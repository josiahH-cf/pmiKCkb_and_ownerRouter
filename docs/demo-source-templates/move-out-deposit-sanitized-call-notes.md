## Why This Matters For The Demo

- Move-outs are a deadline-heavy workflow that can dominate the beginning and end of
  the month.
- The process crosses tenant instructions, owner utility reminders, inspection,
  vendor bids, owner approval, maintenance, deposit disposition, Rentvine financial
  close-out, and re-listing.
- It is a strong KB demo candidate because it shows why placeholders and escalation are
  safer than generic property-management answers when legal wording, charges, or
  deposit decisions are not approved in sources.

## Workflow Facts

- The first and last weeks of the month are often dominated by move-outs.
- When a tenant gives notice, or the team gives notice, the move-out is added to the
  move-out tracking sheet.
- The team sends move-out instructions to the tenant.
- The team sends owner utility reminder communication.
- The team tracks locks/keys, schedules move-out inspection, and follows inspection
  completion.
- The team may need vendor bids, owner approval, maintenance completion, deposit
  disposition, Rentvine move-out steps, re-listing, insurance/Rhino claim handling, or
  collections follow-up depending on the situation.
- The source notes reference a Missouri 30-day window from move-out for security deposit
  handling, but final legal wording and exceptions must be approved before becoming SOP.

## First-Pass Handoff Flow

1. Confirm notice was received or sent and log the move-out in the tracking sheet.
2. Send or confirm tenant move-out instructions using approved wording.
3. Send or confirm the owner utility reminder communication.
4. Track locks, keys, and access needs.
5. Schedule and complete the move-out inspection.
6. Determine whether vendor bids are needed for repairs or cleaning.
7. Route repair scopes, owner approvals, and deposit-sensitive decisions to the
   appropriate approver.
8. Complete approved Rentvine move-out and financial close-out steps.
9. Track re-listing, claims, or collections follow-up only when those steps apply.

## Decision Points

- Has notice been received and logged?
- Have move-out instructions and owner utility reminders been sent?
- Has inspection been scheduled and completed?
- Are vendor bids needed before deposit disposition can be completed?
- Does a bid or repair scope need owner approval?
- Are Rentvine financial close-out steps complete?

## What The KB Can Safely Answer From This Source

- The broad sequence of move-out tracking, instructions, utilities, inspection, vendor
  bids, owner approval, deposit disposition, and Rentvine close-out.
- Why move-out work is deadline-sensitive.
- Which types of questions should be routed to Bailey/Dan or another approver.
- Why the KB should create placeholders for legal wording, charges, or unapproved
  accounting details.

## What The KB Must Not Do From This Source

- Decide deposit disposition.
- Provide final legal notices or deposit language.
- Set or confirm fees, charges, deductions, or owner-billing amounts.
- Approve repairs, vendor bids, claims, collections, or owner charges.
- Update Rentvine, Sheets, inspection records, ledgers, or any system of record.

## Escalation Rules

- Escalate legal deadline uncertainty to Bailey/Dan.
- Escalate repair scopes, owner approvals, deposit disposition questions, claims, or
  collections issues to the responsible approver.
- Escalate missing inspection, vendor, or utility information before drafting a final
  tenant-facing answer.

## Placeholder Triggers

Create a placeholder instead of a confident answer when:

- the question asks for exact legal deposit wording;
- charge amounts or fee details are needed;
- owner approval thresholds are missing;
- inspection findings or vendor bids are not documented;
- Rentvine move-out accounting steps are not approved in a source.
