## Why This Matters For The Demo

- Owner onboarding shows PMI KC's checklist and visibility burden before a property is
  fully operating inside Rentvine.
- The workflow has many prerequisite facts: agreement status, utilities, insurance,
  keys, locks, filters, lawn care, lease takeover details, tenant contact, and deposit
  tracking.
- This is a useful KB demo candidate because the KB can tell staff what to verify and
  where gaps are likely, while refusing to update Rentvine or treat missing onboarding
  facts as known.

## Workflow Facts

- Owner onboarding is tracked in a Google Sheet with many checklist items.
- The onboarding flow can include owner intake, property management agreement status,
  utility identification, additional-insured verification, air filter sizes, keys,
  lock/handle counts, lawn-care assignment, year built, prior lease documents, existing
  tenant contact information, and security deposit tracking.
- The source notes describe many incomplete/red cells in the sheet, suggesting
  visibility gaps when onboarding details are not updated.
- If a property already has a tenant, the team must build the owner, property, and
  tenant information in Rentvine and communicate the management transition to the
  tenant.
- Follow-up may be needed when existing tenants do not respond after onboarding.

## First-Pass Handoff Flow

1. Confirm the owner intake details have been received.
2. Confirm the property management agreement status.
3. Identify utility requirements and any owner utility reminders.
4. Verify additional-insured status and other insurance-related onboarding details.
5. Collect or confirm filter sizes, keys, locks, handles, lawn care, year built, and
   other property setup details.
6. If there is an existing tenant, confirm prior lease documents, tenant contact
   details, deposit status, and transition communication needs.
7. Build or confirm owner, property, and tenant records in Rentvine through the
   responsible human operator.
8. Track missing details in the onboarding sheet or KB placeholders rather than
   assuming they are complete.

## Decision Points

- Has the owner supplied the required onboarding form/details?
- Is the property management agreement signed?
- Are utilities, insurance, keys/locks, lawn care, and filter details known?
- Does the property already have a tenant, and are lease/contact/deposit details known?
- Has Rentvine been built out for the owner, property, and tenant records?
- Is tenant transition wording approved and source-backed?

## What The KB Must Not Do From This Source

- Declare onboarding complete.
- Invent tenant transition wording.
- Confirm deposits, leases, utilities, insurance, or owner obligations without source
  support.

## Escalation Rules

- Escalate missing PM agreement, deposit, lease, utility, or insurance facts to the
  onboarding owner.
- Escalate tenant transition communication gaps to Bailey/Dan before sending.
- Escalate any system-of-record update to the human responsible for Rentvine or the
  onboarding sheet; the KB should not write it.
